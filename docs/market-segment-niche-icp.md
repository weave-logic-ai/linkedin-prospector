Understanding the hierachy of these terms, and how we can help the user narrow the funnel
**Market:** Healthcare
**Segment** A broad grouping inside a market based on shared characteristics. (“Healthcare practices” or “DTC apparel brands”)
**Niche**   A much narrower, targetable slice of a segment with specific business context, pain, and timing.  (“Independent dermatology practices in Texas with 5–15 providers rolling out telehealth”)
**ICP**  The **ideal client profile** inside that niche: the exact person or organization most likely to hire you, get value fast, and be findable. (“Dr. Lisa the growth-minded owner-operator who is overwhelmed by vendor sprawl and compliance risk”)  ICP can also be Ideal Contact Profile, and represent 

### The relationship

Example 1:
* **Market:** Healthcare
* **Segment:** Independent medical practices
* **Niche:** Texas practices with 5–15 providers implementing telehealth
* **ICP:** The physician-owner or practice administrator who can approve a fractional CTO

Example 2:
* **Market:**  Finance
* **Segment:** Wealth management firms
* **Niche:** Boutique wealth managers in the Northeast with $500M–$2B AUM replacing legacy portfolio systems
* **ICP:** COO or founder who owns modernization decisions and is worried about operational risk
* **ICP 2:** Head of Operations who feels the pain daily but may not sign the contract    


### Practical distinction
* Your **niche** answers: **“Where do I play?”**
* Your **ICP** answers: **“Who exactly do I serve there?”**

### Natural Niche Profile
By looking at history a target you can build a full profile of their current and previous Market>Segment>Niche
Previous jobs will have a discernable niche by analyzing the company, and looking at the persons stated experience there.
Niches can be scored and ranked by both the ICP and the offering. 

### Important ICP nuance
There are really two kinds of ICP people often mix together:

1. **Company-level ICP**
   The kind of company that fits best
   Example: “$5M–$20M DTC apparel brands with inventory complexity”

2. **Person-level ICP** matters most, because you need actual humans you can find and speak to
   The individual buyer/decision-maker
   Example: “Founder-CEO who has outgrown founder-led tech decisions”

### The offering
The other piece that is critically related to both the niche and ICP is the offering. The technology and solutions in the offering can help define the niche and ICP. The offering may combine all three in a nice simple package.

### Best practice

Usually the strongest setup is:

* **1 niche**
* **1 primary ICP**
* maybe **1–2 secondary ICPs** if they share the same pain and buying trigger

Too many ICPs usually means the niche is still too broad.


### Scoring your Niche
Score Niches on a Scale of 1-5 (1 = low, 5 = high)
* **Affordability** Can they afford your rates?
* **Fitability** What's Your Level of Experience or Passion?
* **Buildability** Can you build the list, do you have the right network etc?


### A simple test

You likely have a real niche if:

* the group is small enough to name and search for
* they share similar business pains
* they experience similar timing triggers
* the same message would resonate across most of them
* the total pool is roughly **200–2,000 individuals**, not millions
